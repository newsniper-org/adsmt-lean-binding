You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the adsmt source code under
the terms of any **one** of the three licenses above. The three options
exist so downstream users can pick the license that fits their
environment:

  * BSD 2-Clause       — simplest permissive
  * Apache 2.0         — permissive with an explicit patent grant
                         and NOTICE handling
  * LGPL v2.1 or later — weak copyleft, for downstream consumers in
                         (A)GPL/(L)GPL-only environments (e.g. the
                         future logicutils merger)

Why three?
----------

Two design pressures motivate the triple:

  1. The Rust ecosystem expects permissive licensing by default. BSD and
     Apache cover that ground (BSD for projects that prefer the
     two-clause simplicity, Apache for those who want the patent grant).
  2. logicutils — which adsmt will merge with around v1.0 — is intended
     to remain usable inside (A)GPL/(L)GPL-only environments. LGPL v2.1+
     keeps that path open without forcing copyleft on permissive users.
